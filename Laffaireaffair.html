L’affaire Affair.

Mar 30, 2024 Saloni Singh

Events are well-organised chaos!
For as long as I can remember, events have held a magnetic pull on me. Conferences, weddings, fashion shows, award ceremonies – the thrill of the organised chaos, the energy of a crowd coming together for a shared experience – it all filled me with a strange kind of excitement. But it wasn't just about attending these events; a deeper yearning resided within me, a desire to be part of the team that brought them to life. There's a special kind of satisfaction, I realised, that comes from contributing to the creation of something bigger than yourself, of witnessing the meticulous planning transform into a vibrant reality.

This yearning finally found an outlet when I, along with my batchmates, volunteered at the Godrej L'affaire 2024. This wasn't your average event. Godrej L'affaire 2024, held under the simple yet powerful mantra "Conversations. Communities. Collaboration," was a celebration of "All Things Goodness." Encompassing eight distinct zones – Lifestyle, Awareness, Fashion, Flavour, Artistry, Innovation, Rejuvenation, and Environment – the event promised a sensory experience that embodied the spirit of its theme.

Stepping into the world of event management, however, was initially daunting. I remember the time we sat in the briefing room with Zeba Kazi, a senior executive at the Godrej Group. I was very nervous and scared about how I would manage my task. But as she outlined the event's goals and our roles within it, my nervousness melted away, replaced by a burgeoning excitement. We were about to be a part of something special. P.S. Zeba was such a lively person with infectious energy.

The task allocations were a little here and there. To be honest we were a bit confused at the start of the event about what we were actually supposed to do since we were briefed on three different tasks with three different people. But once the event started things and tasks started to fall into place making us function into full fledged working. I think that’s how events work!

One of my primary responsibilities involved content creation. Identifying celebrities and influencers, interacting with them was initially nerve-wracking then became the most fun and interesting part. Learning to navigate these interactions – some brief and courteous, others more in-depth – proved to be a valuable lesson in social agility.

But the most significant takeaway from this experience wasn't limited to influencer interaction or content creation. It was witnessing, obviously not at a full scale, the intricate web of planning and coordination that goes into crafting a large-scale event. Prior to volunteering, I'd only ever seen the finished product – the dazzling lights, the captivating performances, the seamless flow of activities. Now, I had a privileged glimpse behind the scenes, witnessing the tireless efforts and meticulous planning that ensured everything ran like clockwork. It was an eye-opening revelation of the dedication required to pull off such a magnificent feat.

The Godrej team, from Sujit Patil and Michelle Francis to Zeba Kazi, embodied this dedication in its purest form. Even after working tirelessly, their faces remained stamped with vibrant smiles, a testament to their unwavering passion for the event. Their enthusiasm was contagious, further solidifying my own desire to be part of this world.

This experience at Godrej L'affaire 2024 was more than just volunteering; it was kind of a dream come true. It wasn't just about the event itself, but the realisation that my true joy lies in being an architect of experiences, in collaborating with a team to weave together the threads of an idea and bring it to life. I know the work is extremely exhausting but you know what you love might exhaust your body but will never exhaust your soul!
